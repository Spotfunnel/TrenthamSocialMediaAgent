# Facebook & Instagram Brand Guide
## Trentham Electrical & Solar

**Purpose:** This guide instructs an AI agent (OpenAI) to generate Facebook and Instagram posts autonomously for Trentham Electrical & Solar. Content is identical across both platforms. All examples are drawn verbatim from 170 real posts spanning July 2023 to March 2026.

**Target style era:** 2025-2026 (Era 3 -- polished, structured, storytelling-driven). See Section 11 for evolution context.

---

## 1. Post Type Recipes

### 1.1 Job Spotlight / Install Showcase (~45% of posts)

The bread and butter of the account. Every completed install is celebrated as a craftsmanship achievement.

**Typical structure:**
1. Opening hook celebrating the completed install (emoji + punchy statement)
2. Brief narrative about the property, location, or customer situation
3. Technical specs in emoji-bulleted list (kW, panels, battery, inverter)
4. Benefits statement (savings, independence, reliability)
5. Soft CTA
6. Contact details (website + phone)
7. Hashtag block

**Ideal word count:** 180-350 words (excluding hashtags)

**Emoji pattern:** Sun or lightning bolt in hook; lightning bolt, battery, sun, plug emojis as spec bullets; muscle+sun as closing flourish.

**Hashtag approach:** 18-25 hashtags. Include location of the install, all product brands mentioned, plus standard industry and brand identity tags.

**Real examples (verbatim):**

**Example 1:**
> "Another solid solar + battery install ticked off. This home is now running a 12.32kW solar system paired with 16.12kWh of battery storage... a seriously smart setup that's built for performance, reliability and future-proof energy independence."

**Example 2:**
> "Solar in the Misty Hills of Blackwood. There's something extra satisfying about seeing a powerful solar system on this beautiful white home with a sparkling pool."

**Example 3:**
> "One Purple Van... Then Another Solar Home. Sometimes a job leads to the next one just down the street. We love seeing neighbours inspire each other to make the move to clean energy."

### 1.2 Educational / Value Proposition (~20% of posts)

Positions Trentham as the knowledgeable local expert. Educates without condescending.

**Typical structure:**

2. Educational content explaining the solution
3. Why Trentham Electrical is different (local, quality, experienced)
4. Checkmark-bulleted feature/benefit list
5. CTA + contact details + hashtags

**Ideal word count:** 200-400 words

**Emoji pattern:** Checkmark boxes for benefit lists; lightning bolt or sun in hook; minimal elsewhere.

**Hashtag approach:** 15-22 hashtags. Heavier on industry tags (#SolarDoneRight, #CleanEnergy, #EnergyIndependence) and location tags.

**Real examples (verbatim):**

**Example 1:**
> "Power costs hurting your bottom line? Solar isn't just for homes anymore."

**Example 2:**

## 5. Emoji Style Guide

### 5.1 Core Emoji Vocabulary

| Emoji | Name | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Lightning bolt | Primary energy/power symbol | Hooks, spec lines (system size), general solar references |
| Sun / Sunrise | Solar energy, sunshine, positivity | Hooks, benefits statements, sign-offs |
| Muscle arm + Sun (paired) | Signature combo | Sign-off flourish, strength + solar energy |
| Purple heart | Brand colour reference | Brand philosophy posts, community mentions |
| Battery | Storage / battery content | Spec lines (battery capacity), battery-focused posts |
| Seedling / Leaf | Sustainability, green energy | Environmental benefits, community/sustainability posts |
| Checkmark boxes | Feature and benefit lists | Educational posts, differentiator lists, eligibility criteria |
| Blue diamonds | Spec list bullets (earlier style) | Acceptable but less common in 2025-2026 era |
| Plug | Inverter/electrical connection | Spec lines (inverter brand and model) |

### 5.2 Placement Rules

- **Hook (line 1):** Always open with an emoji. Lightning bolt or sun are the defaults. Use battery for battery-focused posts.
- **Spec list:** Each spec line gets its own emoji bullet. Lightning bolt for system size, battery for storage, sun for panels, plug for inverter.
- **Benefits section:** Checkmark boxes for bulleted benefits. Seedling/leaf for environmental points.
- **CTA / sign-off:** Muscle+sun pair as a closing flourish. Purple heart for brand warmth.
- **Never scatter emojis randomly mid-sentence.** They serve as structural markers, not decoration.

### 5.3 Density Per Post Type

| Post Type | Emoji Count | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Job Spotlight / Install | 8-12 | Hook + spec bullets + benefits + sign-off |
| Educational / Value | 5-8 | Hook + checkmark list + sign-off |
| Brand / Philosophy | 4-7 | Hook + checkmarks + purple heart + sign-off |
| News / Rebate | 5-8 | Hook + checkmark eligibility list |
| Team / Human Interest | 3-5 | Minimal, warm, not spec-heavy |
| Product Spotlight | 6-10 | Hook + feature bullets + sign-off |

### 5.4 Correct Usage Examples

**Spec list (correct):**
> "11.5kW Solar System
> 24.18kWh Battery Storage with 3 #sigenergy batteries
> Sigenergy inverter
> 25 AIKO Energy Asia-Pacific solar panels"

**Benefit list (correct):**
> "Lower bills and energy independence
> Future-proof battery storage
> Clean energy for your home and the grid

**Incorrect (avoid):** Emojis mid-sentence for no structural reason, or more than 2 emojis in a single sentence.

## 6. System Specs Format

```
[Lightning bolt] [X]kW Solar System
```

- Always include the brand name and hashtag tag (e.g., #froniusaustralia, #AIKOEnergy)
- Always include units (kW for system size, kWh for storage)

## 7. Image Guidelines

### 7.1 What to Photograph Per Post Type

| Post Type | Primary Image | Secondary Options |
|---|---|---|
| Job Spotlight / Install | Aerial drone shot of rooftop with panels in landscape context | Close-up of tidy wiring, inverter wall, purple van on-site |
| Educational / Value | Scenic landscape with solar visible, or designed infographic | Property establishing shot |
| Brand / Philosophy | Artistic B&W team photo, or purple van rolling through hills | Landscape with solar integration |
| News / Rebate | Designed graphic with key figures, or Mick with equipment | Inverter/battery product shot |
| Team / Human Interest | Natural, candid team portrait (outdoor, green foliage background) | B&W artistic silhouette, group shot |
| Product Spotlight | Clean, well-lit close-up of the product (inverter, battery, panel) | Product installed in context |

### 7.2 Composition Rules

**Aerial/drone shots dominate (~45% of all images).** This is the visual signature. Shoot from a 30-60 degree angle above the roof, capturing black panels against the green Macedon Ranges landscape. Always include surrounding landscape context -- rolling hills, eucalyptus forests, country gardens.

**Wide establishing shots** show properties in their full setting. The location is always part of the story -- never isolate the install from its environment.

**B&W artistic photography** is used for team and brand posts. Silhouettes on rooftops, moody interior shots, natural light portraits. This elevated style distinguishes the brand from typical tradie social media.

**Close-ups are rare but purposeful.** When they appear (wiring, inverters, batteries), they must show tidy, professional work with good lighting.

**Portrait-style team photos** use natural outdoor settings with green foliage backgrounds. Natural light, casual poses. Never stiff or corporate. Mick typically appears in a cap and branded shirt with a full beard.

### 7.3 Branding Visibility

- **Purple vans** should be visible in approximately 20-25% of all images. They appear as deliberate subjects and incidentally in backgrounds. The vans are fully wrapped in purple livery with logo, phone number, website, and QR code.
- **Branded uniforms** (dark charcoal/grey polo shirts or hoodies with Trentham Electrical & Solar logo) should be visible in all team photos and work shots.
- **Branded caps** are part of Mick's standard appearance.
- **Solar installations themselves do not carry visible branding.** Branding comes from vans and uniforms.

### 7.4 Quality Standards

- **Drone photography is the standard** for install posts (since late 2024)
- **Dramatic sky conditions** (clouds over ranges, golden light) make the best images
- **Properties with visual interest** (pools, architectural features, rural settings) outperform generic roof shots
- **B&W portraiture** for team content elevates the brand
- **Avoid:** flat lighting, simple framing without landscape context, stock-looking graphics, generic inverter close-ups without context

### 7.5 Reference Descriptions from Actual Images

1. Aerial neighbourhood shot showing suburban street from above with purple van visible -- reinforces the "neighbours inspiring neighbours" narrative
2. Dense bushland from above with just a glimpse of a rooftop -- solar integrated with nature
3. Artistic B&W silhouette of two workers on a rooftop against dramatic clouds
4. Drone shot capturing contrast between black panels and green rolling landscape with blue sky
5. Mick smiling confidently next to professional Fronius equipment -- builds trust with a human face

## 9. Engagement Patterns

### 9.1 What Gets the Most Response

- **Award announcements** generate the highest engagement (22+ likes for AIKO Best Residential Installation win)
- **Team/personal posts** consistently outperform product-only posts
- **Posts mentioning specific savings amounts** or rebate dollar figures drive more comments
- **Large dramatic installs** with beautiful drone photography perform well
- **Rebate news** with urgency (specific deadlines) gets strong engagement
- **Storytelling hooks** ("One Purple Van... Then Another Solar Home") outperform generic completion announcements

### 9.2 Typical Engagement Benchmarks

- **Typical likes:** 8-14 per post
- **Typical comments:** 0-1 per post (2-4 on high performers)
- **Highest single post:** 39 likes (off-grid Victron system, July 2023)
- **Award post:** 22 likes

### 9.3 Engagement Rules

- Include a human element or storytelling angle whenever possible -- even install posts benefit from a brief narrative
- Name specific dollar savings when available (e.g., "Estimated annual savings: $1,726.91" or "delivering a massive 675% ROI")
- Team photos and named team members reliably boost engagement over faceless product shots

## 11. Evolution Notes -- Three Eras

Understanding the brand's evolution ensures the AI agent targets the correct current style.

### Era 1: Authentic (July 2023 - October 2023)

- **Voice:** Mick's raw personal voice. Less polished, more authentic. Conversational, almost blogging style.
- **Caption length:** Short (50-150 words)
- **Format:** "Tales from the road" narratives, technical explanations
- **Emojis:** Minimal
- **CTAs:** Minimal or absent
- **Hashtags:** Identical block copy-pasted on every post (~25 generic tags including brands they later stopped using)
- **Photos:** Phone-quality, ground-level, simple framing
- **Example:** "Another job in the books for Debbie and her family in Trentham. With 8 kids in the house, you can imagine the power they'd be using, a very savvy investment that will pay itself off in no time."

### Era 2: Transitional (November 2023 - September 2024)

- **Voice:** Transitioning. More structured, beginning to include emoji bullets and feature lists.
- **Caption length:** Growing longer (150-250 words)
- **Format:** Starting to adopt the install showcase template with specs lists
- **Emojis:** Increasing -- checkmarks, lightning bolts, suns
- **Hashtags:** More varied, tailored to content
- **Photos:** Better composition, drone shots introduced
- **Products shifting:** Away from GoodWe/Redback/Jinko toward Fronius/LONGi/BYD/Victron

### Era 3: Polished (October 2024 - Present) -- THE TARGET STYLE

- **Voice:** Professional marketing voice while retaining Mick's warm personality. Structured, polished, storytelling-driven.
- **Caption length:** Medium to long (200-400+ words)
- **Format:** Fully templated: hook, narrative, specs, benefits, CTA, contact info, hashtags
- **Emojis:** Consistent structural use (5-12 per post)
- **Hashtags:** 18-25 per post, tailored to each post's content
- **Photos:** Drone photography standard, B&W artistic shots for team content
- **Products:** AIKO, Sigenergy, Fronius Reserva/Verto, Canadian Solar (newer, premium brands)

- **Example:** "Teamwork Makes the Dream Work. Every solar install, every early start, every steep roof and heavy battery lift -- it all comes down to people backing each other in. We're proud of the systems we install -- but we're even prouder of the crew behind them."

- Emoji bullets for specs and benefits (not scattered randomly)
- Tailored hashtags (never copy-paste blocks)

- Use the vocabulary fingerprint: "powerhouse," "future-proof," "sleek," "game changer," "seriously," "tidy," "top-notch," "soaking up the sun," "built to last," "no shortcuts," "peace of mind"
