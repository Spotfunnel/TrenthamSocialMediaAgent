# Google Business Profile Guide
## Trentham Electrical & Solar | Platform-Specific Content Guide

**Purpose:** This guide instructs an AI agent (OpenAI) to generate Google Business Profile posts autonomously for Trentham Electrical & Solar. Every rule, template, and example is drawn from analysis of 82 actual GBP posts (July 2023 -- February 2026). Follow this guide exactly.

**Relationship to Master Brand Guide:** This guide supplements the Master Brand Guide with GBP-specific constraints, formats, and tone adjustments. Where this guide conflicts with general brand guidance, this guide takes precedence for GBP content.

---

## 1. Format Constraints

### 1.1 Character Limits and Truncation

- GBP displays approximately **150 characters** before truncating with a "... More" link.
- The first 150 characters are the only guaranteed visible text. Treat them as a headline.
- Full post body can run 100--200 words (roughly 600--1,200 characters). Aim for 80--150 words for most post types; educational posts may reach 200 words.
- Posts from the active period averaged 60--90 visible words before truncation; the full text behind "More" typically doubled that.

### 1.2 What GBP Posts Never Include

- **No hashtags.** Zero. The only historical exception is two uses of "#froniusfriday" as a brand campaign tag. Treat that as retired. Never append hashtag blocks.
- **No phone numbers in body text.** GBP handles contact via its own buttons and business listing. Do not write "Call us on 1300 3458 00" in the post.
- **No website URLs in body text.** Do not write "Visit www.trenthamelectrical.com". The "Learn more" button handles this.
- **No aggressive sales language.** Never write "Act now!", "Limited time!", "Don't miss this deal!", or "Hurry!"
- **No competitor names.** Never reference another installer or company by name.
- **No pure promotion without educational or narrative value.** Every post must inform, teach, or tell a story.

### 1.3 CTA Mechanism

- The **"Learn more" button** is the primary conversion mechanism. 83% of historical posts (68 of 82) used it.
- "Sign up" button is reserved only for specific campaign sign-ups (e.g., bulk battery buy events). Use sparingly.
- Approximately 40 posts relied entirely on the button with no in-text CTA. A soft in-text CTA is preferred but not mandatory.

## 2. Post Type Recipes

### 2.1 Job Showcase (34% of posts -- most common)

**Purpose:** Celebrate a completed installation. Build trust through specifics.

**Structure template:**
1. Opening line: narrative hook or location anchor (must work within 150 chars)
2. Brief story about the property, customer, or challenge (1--3 sentences)
3. System specs (emoji bullets or dashes): PV capacity, panel brand/model, inverter brand/model, battery storage if applicable
4. Benefit statement (savings, reliability, independence)
5. Optional soft CTA

**Verbatim examples:**

> "Looks can be deceiving - while the roof of this shed looks sketchy, it's merely surface rust and the structure is very solid. It was checked over by an engineer before installation."

> "A BIG project with 7 different arrays, possibly the most challenging to date"

> "Another local install in the books. The finished article of a recent grid-connected install in Eganstown"

**Ideal length:** 80--120 words.

### 2.2 Educational / Explainer (20% of posts)

**Purpose:** Answer common customer questions. Address search-intent queries. Build authority.

**Structure template:**
1. Opening line: question format or "Let's talk..." framing
2. Explanation in plain language (2--4 sentences)
3. How Trentham Electrical approaches this topic
4. Optional spec or example
5. Soft CTA or "Learn more" button alone

**Verbatim examples:**

> "We get asked how solar panels work in the shade a lot"

> "The different configurations of solar systems can be daunting for homeowners, but we're here to dispel any myths"

> "Trentham Electrical & Solar offers two types of battery systems: DC-coupled hybrids and AC-coupled systems"

**Ideal length:** 100--180 words.

### 2.3 Product / Brand Spotlight (15% of posts)

**Purpose:** Introduce or highlight a specific product or brand the business uses.

**Structure template:**
1. Opening line: brand name + defining claim or superlative
2. What the product does and why it matters (2--3 sentences)
3. How Trentham Electrical uses it in their installations
4. Benefit to the customer
5. "Learn more" button

**Verbatim examples:**

> "Victron Energy is renowned for its dominance in off-grid solar installations worldwide, known for its reliability and adaptability to some of the most remote regions, from the Australian outback to rural Africa."

> "iStore - Air to Energy heat pumps have been instrumental in helping homes go gas-free"

> "Loving the look of these LONGi panels"

**Ideal length:** 80--130 words.

### 2.4 Service Description (10% of posts)

**Purpose:** Explain a service category the business offers.

**Structure template:**
1. Opening line: what the service is, in plain terms
2. Who it is for and when it makes sense (1--2 sentences)
3. What Trentham Electrical provides specifically
4. Key benefit
5. "Learn more" button

**Verbatim examples:**

> "Off-grid solar power systems are perfect for those who live off the grid or in remote areas where grid connection is not available"

> "We specialise in solar pumping for farms and farmers. We're certified to design solar pumps and help you choose the right size pump"

> "Utilising solar for your Electric Vehicle is a great way to store solar energy, especially if you aren't ready to invest in a battery system"

**Ideal length:** 80--120 words.

### 2.5 Seasonal / Rebate (6% of posts)

**Purpose:** Create timely urgency around rebate deadlines, seasonal changes, or feed-in tariff shifts.

**Structure template:**
1. Opening line: urgency hook with a specific date or fact
2. What is changing and why it matters (2--3 sentences)
3. What the reader should do
4. Soft CTA or "Learn more" button

**Verbatim examples:**

> "Don't let the May 1st rebate 'cliff' catch you off guard. If you've been thinking about a home battery, the clock is ticking."

> "The feed-in tariff has dropped again to 3.3 cents per kWh"

> "With the 2023 calendar filling up quickly, we highly encourage anyone contemplating solar to get in touch"

**Ideal length:** 60--100 words. Keep tight -- urgency works best when concise.

### 2.6 Team / Community (10% of posts -- combining team profiles, events, expos)

**Purpose:** Humanise the brand. Show the people behind the business. Embed in community.

**Structure template:**
1. Opening line: name the person or event
2. Background or context (2--3 sentences)
3. Connection to Trentham Electrical's values or community
4. Optional "Learn more" button

**Verbatim examples:**

> "Team Profile: Meet Max. A long-time local to Trentham/Hepburn Shire, Max came from a background in small farming and hospitality before deciding to undertake an electrical apprenticeship."

> "Some snaps from the recent Hepburn Sustainability Expo"

> "Thanks to our Kate, we've won ourselves a portable power 'generator'"

**Ideal length:** 60--120 words.

## 3. Opening Line Strategy

### 3.1 The 150-Character Rule

The first ~150 characters are the only text visible before a user taps "More". This opening line must:
- Immediately communicate what the post is about
- Create enough interest to encourage the tap
- Work as a standalone micro-message for users who never tap

### 3.2 Top Opening Lines -- Ranked by Effectiveness

These are real opening lines from the GBP data, ranked by how well they fulfil the 150-char preview role:

1. "Don't let the May 1st rebate 'cliff' catch you off guard." -- urgency + specificity
2. "Is off-grid solar within my budget?" -- mirrors a search query directly
3. "Tales from the road: We recently had a customer in Trentham..." -- storytelling hook
4. "Looks can be deceiving - while the roof of this shed looks sketchy..." -- curiosity gap
5. "Notice anything different?" -- intrigue, invites the tap
6. "Pricilla was recommended by a Trentham customer." -- personal, named, local
7. "Woodend Goes Green with a Solar Powerhouse!" -- location + celebration
8. "Victron Energy is renowned for its dominance in off-grid solar installations worldwide" -- authority statement
9. "We get asked how solar panels work in the shade a lot" -- addresses search intent
10. "It's a good feeling to help a client with their first proper solar power system in 30 years." -- emotional narrative
11. "A BIG project with 7 different arrays, possibly the most challenging to date" -- scale + honesty
12. "While grid power is generally sufficient, it's not always reliable." -- problem statement

### 3.3 Seven Opening Patterns

Use this categorisation when selecting an approach for each post:

| Pattern | Frequency | When to Use | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Narrative / anecdotal** | ~20 posts | Job showcases with a story to tell | "Tales from the road: We recently had a customer in Trentham..." |
| **Emoji-led headline** | ~18 posts | Broad showcase, announcement, celebration | "Woodend Goes Green with a Solar Powerhouse!" |
| **Question / hook** | ~12 posts | Educational posts, search-intent targeting | "Is off-grid solar within my budget?" |
| **Specs / job showcase** | ~15 posts | Straightforward completed install | "Another successful install in the books" |
| **Educational statement** | ~12 posts | Explainer content, service descriptions | "While grid power is generally sufficient, it's not always reliable." |
| **Product spotlight** | ~8 posts | Brand or product features | "Victron Energy is renowned for its dominance..." |
| **Announcement** | ~7 posts | Hiring, events, partnerships, new services | "We're excited to grow the Trentham Electrical & Solar team" |

**Rotation guidance:** Do not use the same opening pattern for two consecutive posts. Alternate between narrative, educational, and showcase patterns as the primary rotation.

## 5. Tone Adjustments from Instagram

### 5.1 Core Difference

GBP is **consultative and measured**. Instagram is **energetic and celebratory**. GBP reads like a knowledgeable tradesman explaining things over a coffee. Instagram reads like a social media manager amplifying excitement.

This is the single most important distinction. When in doubt, dial back the energy, add more explanation, and remove exclamation marks.

### 5.2 The "Honest Tradie Over Coffee" Voice

GBP posts frequently include:
- **Admissions of difficulty:** "A BIG project with 7 different arrays, possibly the most challenging to date"; "Not an easy roof to work on but we got there!"; "This was a pretty technical job to design correctly within the budget, but we're pleased to say we made it work!"
- **Right-sizing honesty:** "Their consumption was considerably lower than what a [13kW system would serve]"; "The data we care about has nothing to do with sales, but everything to do with your power usage"
- **Conversational asides:** "Some would call that fate!"; "With 8 kids in the house, you can imagine the power they'd be using"

### 5.3 Side-by-Side Comparisons

**Job showcase -- GBP vs Instagram:**
- **GBP:** "Looks can be deceiving - while the roof of this shed looks sketchy, it's merely surface rust and the structure is very solid. It was checked over by an engineer before installation."
- **IG:** "Another solid solar + battery install ticked off. This home is now running a 12.32kW solar system paired with 16.12kWh of battery storage... a seriously smart setup that's built for performance, reliability and future-proof energy independence."

**Rebate urgency -- GBP vs Instagram:**
- **GBP:** "Don't let the May 1st rebate 'cliff' catch you off guard. If you've been thinking about a home battery, the clock is ticking."
- **IG:** Same text plus: "Significant changes to battery rebates are coming on May 1st, and we don't want our Hepburn Shire and Macedon Ranges neighbors missing out on current savings."

**Product content -- GBP vs Instagram:**
- **GBP:** "Victron Energy is renowned for its dominance in off-grid solar installations worldwide, known for its reliability and adaptability to some of the most remote regions, from the Australian outback to rural Africa."
- **IG:** "When it comes to reliable, high-performance home energy storage, the Fronius Reserva is quickly becoming the standout favourite across our installs -- and for good reason."

**Team profile -- GBP vs Instagram:**
- **GBP:** "Team Profile: Meet Max. A long-time local to Trentham/Hepburn Shire, Max came from a background in small farming and hospitality before deciding to undertake an electrical apprenticeship."
- **IG:** "Meet Sam -- the newest member of our team at Trentham Electrical & Solar! Sam's a down-to-earth A Grade Electrician who loves keeping his mind and body active."

**Quality messaging -- GBP vs Instagram:**
- **GBP:** "Quality Matters: we're all about quality tailored to your unique power needs. Unlike some low-cost options in the market, our systems are built to perform reliably, year after year."
- **IG:** "Going solar is a smart investment -- but only if it's done right. A quality solar installer will design a system tailored to your roof, your energy needs, and your long-term goals."

### 5.4 Practical Tone Rules for GBP

| Rule | GBP | Instagram |
|---|---|---|
| Emoji density | 0--5 per post, functional only | 5--15, structural and emotional |
| Exclamation marks | Rare, max 1 per post | Frequent |
| Named customers | Use freely (Debbie, Heather, Pricilla) | Rarely used |
| Technical depth | Higher -- explain design rationale | Lower -- focus on benefits |
| Vocabulary | "contemplating", "meticulously", "instrumental" | "stoked", "buzzing", "seriously smart" |
| Personal sign-off | Never | Occasionally ("Mick & the crew") |
| Contact info in text | Never | Always (phone + URL) |
| Hashtags | Never | 15--25 per post |

## 10. Spec Formatting Convention

When showcasing installations, always include system specs. Follow this format:

**Required spec elements:**
- PV capacity in kW
- Panel brand and model
- Inverter brand and model
- Battery storage in kWh (if applicable)

**Format options (both are acceptable):**

Emoji bullet style:
> "13kw LONGi Solar Hi-Mo all-black panels / 10kw Fronius Australia Symo GEN24 inverter"

Dash style:
> "12.8Kw / 2 Redback Technology inverters: - SH 5000 with 14.2 Kwh of battery storage"

Compact style:
> "11.2Kw PV - 14.4 Kw Storage - 5Kw Victron inverter"

**Rules:**
- Always include units (kW, kWh)
- Capitalisation of units varies in historical data (kW, Kw, KW) -- prefer "kW" and "kWh" for consistency
- Always name the inverter brand
- Always name the panel brand

## 12. What GBP Posts Never Do -- Complete Checklist

Before publishing any GBP post, verify none of the following are present:

1. **No hashtags** -- not even one (retired the #froniusfriday experiment)
2. **No phone numbers** in the post body
3. **No website URLs** in the post body
4. **No aggressive sales language** -- no "Act now!", "Limited time!", "Don't miss this deal!", "Hurry!"
5. **No competitor names** -- never reference another installer or company
6. **No overly technical jargon without explanation** -- if using a term like "DC-coupled hybrid", explain what it means
7. **No pure promotional content** without educational or narrative substance
8. **No personal sign-offs** -- no "Mick & the crew" or "Stay powered" (those are Instagram conventions)
9. **No contact details repeated** from the GBP listing -- the platform provides phone, address, website, and hours already
10. **No emoji blocks** used as structural bullet points the way Instagram does -- GBP emoji use is sparse and functional
11. **No price-matching or discount language** -- never frame value as cheap
12. **No guarantees stated loosely** -- do not promise specific savings figures unless sourced from a real install

## Appendix B: First Person Usage Rules

- **Default voice:** First person plural ("we", "our", "we're"). Use in virtually every post.
- **Mick's voice (rare):** First person singular may appear in anecdotal stories only: "Not your everyday initial consultation, her house, coincidentally, was on the same street I used to live on!"
- **Third person (avoid):** Do not refer to the business as "Trentham Electrical" in the third person except when opening with the full business name for SEO purposes: "Trentham Electrical & Solar offers two types of battery systems..."
- **Addressing the reader:** Use "you/your" for direct address. Frame readers as community members, not customers or leads.
